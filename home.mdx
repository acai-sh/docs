## **Very Concise Summary**

The toolkit is centered around **Feature Requirements** (AKA, Acceptance Criteria), written in plain language in a very simple .yaml file.

It **automatically syncs and tracks requirements** across your project, so you can ask:

- Which requirements have test coverage?
- Which requirements have changed, are being implemented, or need attention?
- Which parts of my codebase are related to this requirement?
- Which requirements have passed QA and engineering review?

It does this via a **command-line tool and dashboard UI**.

These tools help you to:

- Rapidly iterate by adding and changing requirements at the spec level.
- Maintain specifications that actually reflect reality.

It is open source, and works for any tech stack, and any software product (frontend, backend, CLI, SDK, API, microservice, monolith, etc.)

# Why ACAI? Why now?

Thanks to LLMs, the tedium of writing, referencing, and testing ACs has been completely eliminated.

None of these ideas are new, but they were far too cumbersome for most teams.

ACAI is purpose built to serve as part of a larger agentic software development workflow. You've probably already observed that AI agents (at least, today's iterations) LOVE to self-reference their prompt and track requirements.

Giving them stable identifiers to track improves the quality of their output, and gives you a new window into their rationale. Next time your agent goes off the rails, you will be able course-correct much quicker.
