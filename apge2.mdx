Acai is a toolkit for spec-driven software development. Acai makes it possible to ship _better quality_ and _more ambitious_ software, while minimizing slop.

**Very Concise Summary**

It includes a command-line tool and dashboard UI.

The toolkit is centered around _Acceptance Criteria_, also known as **Feature Requirements.**

It automatically tracks requirements across your code, so you can ask:

- Which requirements have test coverage?
- Which requirements have changed, are being implemented, or need attention?
- Which parts of my codebase are related to this requirement?
- Which requirements have passed QA and engineering review?

It helps you rapidly iterate, by making it easy to do things like:

- Maintain source-of-truth product specs that actually reflect reality
- Add, change, or deprecate requirements, then dispatch agents to resolve the changes

It is open source, and works for any tech stack, and any software product (frontend, backend, CLI, SDK, API, microservice, monolith, etc.)

## The Mapperoni Method

Acai was built to facilitate a specific _process_ of software development called Mapperoni Method. It is called the Mapperoni Method because it is the process that evolved while we were building [mapperoni.com](https://www.mapperoni.com).
